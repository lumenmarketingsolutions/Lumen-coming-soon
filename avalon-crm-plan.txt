THE CORE PAIN
-------------
They are drowning in fragmented tools. Every single person said the same thing different ways:

- Kristen: "scattered Google Docs... personal WhatsApp... can't find information... clicking in and out of platforms"
- Laleh: "having to go back to Boulevard to send messages"
- Jessica: "wish it had more integration and seamlessness... one all encompassing platform"
- Bre: "notes and communication within profiles that you can see no matter what location"

The #1 problem is not leads. It's operational fragmentation across locations. They're managing patient journeys, internal comms, lead follow-up, notes, quotes, and scheduling across Moxie, Boulevard, WhatsApp, Google Docs, email, and phone — separately per location.


WHAT MOXIE FAILED AT
---------------------
- Leads were sparse and low quality
- Platform was hard to navigate even for tech-savvy users (Kristen's words)
- No real onboarding or training
- Slow support, pushback when asked for help
- No real Boulevard integration
- GHL (Go High Level) was confusing, Moxie didn't help them learn it

This is our biggest advantage. They've been burned by a platform that promised integration and delivered fragmentation. We deliver the opposite.

THE PERSONAS — WHAT EACH CARES ABOUT MOST
-------------------------------------------

Bre (RDOO)
Needs the 30,000ft view. Dashboard showing all locations, lead counts, team activity. She doesn't need to text patients herself, she needs to see that it's happening.

Kristen (Ops/Marketing)
Needs internal comms fixed. WhatsApp and Google Docs are killing her. Wants Zoho-level organization. She referenced Zoho CRM twice — she's done her own research. She wants one platform for patient AND internal workflows.

THE HARD PARTS
--------------

1. Boulevard Integration
Everyone wants leads to flow into BLVD. We know API/Zapier is possible but this is the most technically complex piece. We need to nail the sync direction: leads come into our CRM > when booked, push to BLVD as a client.

2. Multi-Location Architecture
Every user needs to see their location's data AND cross-location data. Bre and Kristen need global views. Laleh and Jessica need location-scoped views. Role-based permissions are essential.

3. SMS/Twilio
Laleh specifically said she wants to text from the platform. We already have Twilio creds. But two-way SMS with conversation threading is a real build.

4. Internal Chat
Kristen and Bre want team messaging. This replaces WhatsApp. Needs to be per-location AND global. This is a significant feature but would be a massive differentiator.

5. Quotes/Invoicing
Kristen wants professional digital quotes. Laleh wants payment links. This is scope creep territory — plan for it but potentially Phase 2.


WHAT WOULD BLOW THEM AWAY ON FIRST LOGIN
------------------------------------------

1. Dashboard that shows all 3+ locations at a glance — lead counts, pipeline stages, team activity, today's tasks. Bre sees this and immediately knows we understood her role.

2. Lead pipeline with drag-and-drop stages — Laleh logs in, sees her leads in columns (New > Contacted > Consultation > Booked > Won), drags a card, adds a note. She feels like she's using Salesforce but it's clean and simple.

3. Patient/lead profiles with full history — Every note, text, quote, touchpoint on one page. Cross-location visible. Kristen sees this and knows the Google Docs era is over.

4. One-click text from a lead profile — Laleh hits "text" on a lead card, types, sends. It goes via Twilio. The conversation threads right there on the profile. She never leaves the CRM.

5. Internal notes vs. patient-facing comms clearly separated — Team notes stay internal. Patient texts are patient texts. No more WhatsApp confusion.


BUILD PLAN
----------

Phase 1 — Core (Week 1-2)
- Auth system (role-based: RDOO, Manager, Staff)
- Multi-location data model
- Lead pipeline (stages, drag-drop, notes)

Phase 2 — Communication (Week 2-3)

- Auto first-touch text on new lead
- Internal team notes on profiles

Phase 3 — Integration & Polish (Week 3-4)
- Boulevard sync (lead > client push)
- Appointment reminders (auto)
- Email from platform
- Quotes/invoicing (basic)
- Mobile-responsive polish
