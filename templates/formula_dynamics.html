What We Found

Six things, looking at the surface.

Once we are inside Klaviyo, Shopify, Google, and Meta the picture sharpens, but these six are what stand out today.

01
Working · do not touch

You own your branded SERP and your proprietary SKUs.

Search "Formula Dynamics" and your homepage, collections, About, and Contact occupy the entire first page. You also rank number one organically for proprietary product names like the Drive By Wire Enhancement Module and Larini Systems Maserati distributor. 237 keywords ranking total, with organic supplying 74 percent of your search driven traffic.

Protecting this means every page that already ranks stays exactly as it is, including titles, URLs, and on page copy. We bid defensively on your brand name in paid search so competitors cannot buy the spot above your organic listing, and we watch the rankings every month so any drop is caught the same month it happens.

02
Bleeding · immediate fix

You are invisible on category head terms.

The buyer searching Maserati GranTurismo exhaust upgrade or Maserati performance parts sees Fabspeed, Fi Exhaust, Supersprint, Scuderia Car Parts, Performance SpeedShop, CARiD, NAPA. Formula Dynamics is not on page one for any of these. Each of those clicks is a buyer who should have landed in your store, and they are landing in your competitor's instead. This is the most expensive gap on the audit.

03
Bleeding · immediate cut

Display is your largest paid channel and the sessions are four seconds.

Public traffic data shows Display as your primary inbound source today, with an average visit duration of four seconds. That is bot adjacent traffic, almost certainly placed by a network optimizing for impressions rather than buyers of $9,500 brake kits. The money is better spent on Search head term coverage and YouTube pre rolls in front of actual exotic owners.

04
Bleeding · build it

Your organic Meta is the only thing running.

The Instagram is consistent, the Page is active, the posts look the part. The gap is the paid layer that turns those impressions into buyers. The play that wins for a brand selling $9,500 brake kits is audience architecture built page by page, where a visitor who lands on a Maserati exhaust gets retargeted with that exhaust and a buyer who priced a Brembo BBK sees that BBK again with the install video and the price. Right now the buyers who already touched your site are walking away cold.

05
Bleeding · immediate fix

Your welcome email is broken, and I tested it myself.

I signed up the same way any visitor would. The footer form promised Discounts straight to your inbox. What arrived, fifteen minutes later, was a welcome email titled Let's Get This Build Started, with no discount code and no offer. The only clickable items in the email were three product tiles pointed at specific Maserati oil change kits and wheel bolts, which assumes the reader is shopping for those exact things and writes off everyone else.

The body opens with No BS, Just Performance, which lands empty for a buyer who already trusted you enough to give you their email. The subject line implies the reader has not started their build yet, and for anyone who has actually been working on their car for years the gut reaction is I have been at this forever, which is friction at the first impression. It also misses anyone replacing a part, upgrading what they already have, or shopping for the next thing rather than the first thing. The whole email is written about Formula Dynamics, when it needs to be written about what the buyer came in to find.

A visitor who signs up for a discount needs the code in their inbox within thirty seconds, in a subject line that matches the offer that made them sign up. If the front door is this broken, the abandoned cart, browse abandon, post purchase, and win back flows behind it are almost certainly missing the same way.

06
Hidden moat

You have the most ownable B2B angle in this category, and no one knows.

You said the brand already has real B2B opportunity flowing. From the outside, none of it is visible. There is no way for a shop owner, installer, or fleet buyer to learn what you offer them, and no path for them to apply for a trade account.

There are two losses sitting in that gap: the dealer pipeline that walks past your site every day with nowhere to land, and the perception lift you miss when a brand at your level has no visible dealer program. A buyer choosing between three sites for a $9,500 brake kit picks the one other shops also trust.

Your position as the exclusive North American distributor for Larini Systems is the strongest B2B hook we have ever audited at this scale, and right now no one outside your shop knows it exists.

The Hidden Moat

You are the exclusive North American distributor for Larini Systems. Every other shop in this country that wants to sell Larini has to come through you. That single fact is the most ownable B2B angle in the category.

A dedicated dealer landing page anchored on this fact is the bonus opportunity in this proposal. We will detail it in the tier breakdown below.

The Strategy

Three pillars, built to compound.

The system works in three pillars: we acquire the buyer who is already searching, retarget the buyer who has already shown intent, and retain the buyer who has already paid. Each pillar feeds the next, and the engine gets cheaper to run every month it stays on.

I.

Acquire

Google Search on category head terms (Maserati GranTurismo exhaust, Ferrari 488 brake upgrade, Levante tuning ECU). Shopping ads on the full catalog feed. Competitor keyword conquest against Fabspeed, Fi Exhaust, Scuderia. YouTube pre-rolls in front of exotic-owner audiences.

II.

Retarget

Audience architecture built page by page inside Meta, so a visitor who looked at a Maserati exhaust gets Maserati exhaust ads, and a buyer who priced a Brembo big brake kit sees that exact kit again with the install context and the price. The system always serves what the buyer already showed they wanted.

III.

Retain

A full Klaviyo build covering welcome, browse abandonment, abandoned cart, post purchase, and win back. Every subject line and every body copy line written by someone who can speak the language of an exotic owner. The list becomes the asset that outlasts any individual ad campaign.

Once a buyer lands on a $9,500 Brembo big brake kit, the next ad they see needs to be that exact kit, with the install context and the price, while the iron is still hot.
