Why this doesn't happen at $10K

This is not an app. This is a full AI-powered business operating system. CRM automation, content generation engine, lead management, email marketing, social media automation, paid ad management, direct mail coordination, team dashboards, and a daily AI briefing system. That is not one project. That is eight different products wired together.

  • 15+ integrations that each need to work reliably. Lofty CRM, Slack, Todoist, Trello, Gmail or Outlook, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, Google Business Profile, Meta Ads API, Google Ads API, HeyGen, MLS/IDX, Reddit, and a direct mail vendor. Every single one of those is a connection that needs authentication, error handling, and monitoring. When one breaks, the whole workflow that depends on it stops.
  • Lofty CRM has a limited API. Half the tasks on this list depend on syncing with Lofty. Creating contacts, moving pipeline stages, triggering automations, doing a full data migration. Lofty does not make all of that easy or even possible through their API. You would not know that until you are already deep into the build. That alone could burn 20+ hours of dev time just discovering what you can and cannot do.
  • Meta and Google ad automation requires API approval. You cannot just plug into Meta Ads or Google Ads and start building audiences and rotating creatives. Both platforms have approval processes, rate limits, and compliance requirements. This is its own project, not a line item.
  • Daily AI content generation has real ongoing costs. Generating posts, emails, captions, briefs, and follow-ups every single day across multiple platforms means hundreds of LLM API calls daily. That runs $200 to $500 per month just in token costs before you even talk about the engineering to keep it running.
  • A voice-to-content pipeline is a product, not a feature. Voice note in, transcribe, detect intent, output emails, social posts, captions, YouTube ideas, stories. That is speech-to-text, intent classification, multi-format content generation, platform-specific formatting, and an approval queue. Companies charge $50 to $200 per month just for this one capability.

At $10,000, whoever takes this on is either planning to build something that demos well and breaks in production, or they do not understand the scope of what they are agreeing to. Either way, Brian is the one holding the risk.
