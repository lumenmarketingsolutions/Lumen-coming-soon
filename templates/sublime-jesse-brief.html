Here's what I found on Sublime, and what they need to hear.

I went through their entire brand. Website, products, pricing, positioning. The good news is that the product is solid and the price points are right. They are genuinely close to their $100K goal. The things holding them back are fixable and most of them are fast.

What to tell them

Keep it simple. The core message is: your product is good, but the way it's being presented online is not doing it justice, and that gap is where all the lost revenue is sitting.

Here are the main points to hit:

  • The website needs to match the product. Their logo is great. The site around it is not. Their buyers have shopped on Sephora and Ulta. That is the bar, whether they realize it or not. The current site is leaving money on the table every single day.
  • The path from seeing the product to buying it has too many steps. When someone clicks a link from a video, they need to be 1-2 taps from checkout. Right now there is too much in between.
  • The domain name is hurting them. "Tallow" means nothing to most people. Something like "Sublime Apothecary" would click immediately. Simple change, big impact.
  • Product photos need to be elevated. In skincare, the image is the product. The current photos are inconsistent and don't communicate the quality that's actually in the bottle.
  • There is a specific play with UGC creators that would put their products in front of millions of people within the first few weeks. This is how unknown brands break through the trust barrier.

The tone to use: confident, direct, and encouraging. They are close. This is not a "your brand is broken" conversation. It's a "you're a few changes away from where you want to be, and here's exactly what those changes are" conversation.
