The Business

You are sitting on something most companies spend years trying to build.

Over 100,000 SKUs in inventory, custom band saw blades made in house, military and aerospace grade clamps and fasteners, and a family owned operation with real people answering the phone. That combination of depth, capability, and personal service is rare in industrial supply, and it is a real competitive advantage.

You have both a DTC channel and a B2B channel, with the aviation division being the highest margin vertical. You specialize in custom hardware and custom packaging, and those are the kind of differentiators that should be front and center in everything a potential buyer sees. Right now they are buried.

The product catalog, the capabilities, and the expertise are all there. What is missing is the digital infrastructure to make sure the right buyers can find you, trust you, and purchase from you without friction.

Visibility

Your strongest offerings are invisible to the people who need them most.

Critical

Your custom capabilities are scattered and buried across the site.

There is a "Custom Made to Order Services" page on the site, but it funnels directly into a band saw blade order form. The intro text mentions lockwiring, specialty coatings, passivation, dry film lubricant, black oxide coating, and zinc flake coating, and all of those services are compressed into a single sentence above a form that only addresses one product. There is also a link in the footer to "Custom Hex/Specialty Nuts Made to Print" that most visitors will never find. Custom packaging is not mentioned anywhere on the site. These are serious capabilities that are being treated like footnotes. Each of these services deserves its own section on a dedicated custom capabilities page with descriptions, examples of past work, and a clear inquiry form. This should be in the main navigation and featured on the homepage because it is the single biggest differentiator this business has, and right now a buyer would have to dig through the footer to even know it exists.

Critical

There is no dedicated page for aviation and aerospace buyers.

You carry military and aerospace grade clamps and fasteners in materials like A286, titanium, and monel. This is the highest margin vertical in your business, and there is nothing on the site that speaks directly to an aviation procurement team. There are no certifications displayed, no CAGE code, no compliance callouts, and no case studies showing what you have supplied to companies in this space. An aviation buyer evaluating suppliers will move on in seconds if they do not see the credentials they need. A single industry page with the right language and the right trust signals would change that entirely.

Critical

SEO is completely untouched.

The homepage meta description is empty and the page title literally says "Home Page." The blog has two posts with no dates and no real substance. For a company with over 100,000 SKUs, there should be hundreds of product searches driving organic traffic to the site every single day. Right now someone searching for "custom band saw blades" or "aerospace hose clamps" has no way to find you through Google. That is traffic from buyers who are actively looking to purchase, and it is going to your competitors instead.

Trust

B2B buyers need proof before they spend serious money.

Critical

The quote request goes to a restricted Google Form.

For a B2B company where the quote is the primary conversion action, this is the equivalent of a broken checkout button on an ecommerce store. A procurement manager requesting a quote on a $50,000 order of aerospace fasteners should not be landing on a Google Form. This needs to be a clean, branded form on the actual website that captures company name, industry, part numbers, and volume. It needs to feel like a professional operation because that is exactly what your buyers expect when they are evaluating a new supplier.

High Impact

Zero social proof anywhere on the site.

There are no customer reviews, testimonials, client logos, or case studies anywhere on the site. The about page talks about family owned values and 100,000 products in inventory, but there is nothing from an actual customer validating that experience. B2B buyers making large purchasing decisions need to see that other companies have trusted you with their business before they will trust you with theirs. Even a handful of strong testimonials from real clients would dramatically change how a new visitor perceives the operation.

High Impact

No certifications or compliance credentials are displayed.

You sell military and aerospace grade products but the site does not display any ISO certifications, CAGE codes, or compliance documentation. For aviation procurement teams these credentials are requirements, not nice to haves. If a buyer cannot find your certifications within a few seconds of landing on your site they will assume you do not have them and move on. These need to be visible and prominent, especially on any page targeting aerospace or defense buyers.

Conversion

The site is built to browse but not to buy.

High Impact

The B2B onboarding path sends buyers to a Google Form.

There is a small "Get B2B Quote" button at the top of the site and it links out to an external Google Form. For a company selling to aviation and aerospace procurement teams this is a credibility problem. A buyer evaluating you as a potential supplier is going to see that Google Form and immediately question the professionalism of the entire operation. It does not match the level of business you are trying to attract. On top of that, there is no information about net terms, volume pricing tiers, or how to set up an ongoing supply account. The B2B entry point needs to be a branded experience on your actual website that makes a buyer feel like they are working with a serious supplier.

High Impact

The homepage is not prioritizing the right products.

The featured products on the homepage right now are split lock washers and hex cap screws. Those are commodity items that every hardware supplier carries. Your homepage should be leading with the things that make you different, like the custom band saw blades you make in house, your aerospace grade fasteners, and your custom hardware and packaging capabilities. The first thing a visitor sees should be the thing that nobody else can offer them. That is what stops someone from clicking back to the next search result.

Opportunity

The blog and content strategy are dormant.

There are two blog posts on the site, neither one has a date, and neither one has the depth to rank for anything in search. For a company with this much product expertise there is a massive opportunity to create content that answers the exact questions your buyers are already typing into Google. Things like "what clamp type do I need for fuel injection" or "A286 vs 316 stainless for aerospace applications" or "how to spec a custom band saw blade." Every one of those articles is a page that can rank and bring a buyer directly to your site who is ready to purchase.

The foundation of this business is strong. You have over 100,000 products, in house manufacturing capability, and the expertise to serve aerospace and defense. The digital infrastructure just needs to catch up to the business you have already built. The buyers are out there searching for exactly what you sell, and they just cannot find you yet.

The Play

What a real growth engine looks like for this business.

Two tracks running at the same time. Fix the site so it converts, and build the visibility so the right buyers can find you.

Track 1: Fix the conversion path
  1. Replace the Google Form with a real quote system.
    Build a branded quote request form on the actual website that captures company name, industry, part numbers, quantities, and timeline. This form should send an instant confirmation to the buyer and a notification to your sales team with everything they need to respond. The quote request is the most important conversion action on this entire site and it needs to feel like it.
  2. Consolidate all custom capabilities into one strong page.
    Take everything that is currently scattered across the site, the band saw blade custom orders, the lockwiring, the specialty coatings, the passivation, the custom hex nuts made to print, the custom packaging, and put it all on one dedicated capabilities page. Each service gets its own section with a clear description, examples of past work, and photos. One inquiry form at the bottom for buyers to describe what they need. This page goes in the main navigation and gets featured on the homepage. It becomes the entry point for every buyer who needs something custom built, which is the entire reason most of these buyers would choose you over a commodity supplier.
  3. Build an aviation and aerospace industry page.
    A dedicated page that speaks directly to procurement teams. Display certifications, materials capabilities, compliance documentation, and any past contracts or case studies you can share. Use the language that aerospace buyers expect to see. This page is how you win the highest margin accounts in your business.
  4. Create a real B2B account onboarding flow.
    An "Open a Business Account" page that outlines net terms, volume pricing, dedicated support, and a fast application process. Make it easy for a business to start buying from you on an ongoing basis. This is what turns a one time order into a recurring revenue relationship, and right now that entire path is a Google Form.
Track 2: Build the visibility
  1. SEO and content that ranks for buyer intent searches.
    Fix the meta descriptions, page titles, and product schema across the entire site. Then build out content that answers the questions your buyers are already typing into Google, like product comparison guides, material specification articles, and breakdowns of which products to use for specific applications. Each piece of content becomes a page that ranks and brings in a buyer who is actively looking for what you sell. With over 100,000 SKUs the search opportunity here is enormous.
  2. Collect and display social proof.
    Reach out to your existing customers and ask for testimonials. Get Google reviews. If you have worked with recognizable companies, get permission to display their logos and add a "Trusted By" section to the homepage and the aerospace page. Social proof is what turns someone who is still evaluating into someone who actually commits to purchasing.
  3. Targeted outreach to aviation procurement teams.
    Once the aerospace page is built and the credentials are displayed, you have something worth putting in front of the right people. A direct outreach campaign to aviation maintenance companies, MRO facilities, and defense contractors with a link to your capabilities page and a clear quote request path. This is where the high value B2B contracts come from.
