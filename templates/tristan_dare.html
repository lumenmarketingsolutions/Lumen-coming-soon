03 / Your Tier System

The Framework is Right. Let Me Show You How It Operates.

Your three tier structure is exactly how luxury brands create both accessibility and exclusivity at the same time. You called them C tier, B tier, and A tier in your document. Here is how I would build the system around each one.

C Tier / Always Accessible

Meteorite Rings

$249 to $549. Always available on the site. This is the entry point. Someone discovers you on Reddit or Instagram, they are not going to buy a $15,000 knife on their first visit. But they will buy a $350 ring. This is how you convert attention into customers. Once someone owns a piece of your work, they are in your world. They get the emails. They see the drops. They move up when they are ready.

B Tier / Drops

Folding Knives, EDC, Accessories

$1,500 to $5,000+. Released every two months. Limited quantity. This is where the drop model lives. You announce it two weeks out. You build the anticipation through email and content. The drop goes live at a specific time. They sell out. That cycle repeating every two months creates a rhythm that your audience starts to expect and plan for. The scarcity is real because you physically cannot make more than a handful at a time.

A Tier / Commissions

One of One Pieces

$10,000 to $100,000+. By application only. These are the pieces that get talked about. The ones that end up in collections. The ones that create press. You do not sell these through a checkout button. You sell these through relationships. The website should have a section dedicated to past commissions with photography that makes people stop scrolling. The inquiry form should feel exclusive because it is.

Your document had this mapped out already. What it was missing was the mechanics of how each tier feeds the next one. A ring buyer becomes a drop follower. A drop buyer becomes a commission inquirer. That is not just a product ladder. That is a customer journey, and the website and email system need to be built to guide people through it.

05 / The Drop System

How a Drop Actually Works When It Is Built Right

You mentioned in your document that you want to run drops every two months. That cadence is solid. Here is what a single drop cycle looks like when the system is in place.

  1. 1 14 days out. The first email goes to the full list announcing that a drop is coming. You tease the pieces. You show a detail shot or two. You do not reveal everything. You tell them the date and time. This email also goes out as social content. A short video of you at the forge, working on something. No hard sell. Just "something is coming."
  2. 2 7 days out. The second wave. Full product reveal. Photography. Pricing. Quantities. This is where you show them exactly what is dropping and how many there are. The scarcity is real, so you just state the numbers. "Four pieces. Here they are. Next Thursday at 12pm PST." This email converts casual followers into people who set alarms.
  3. 3 24 hours out. The reminder. Short. Direct. "Tomorrow at noon. Here is the link. Do not be late." This is the one that creates urgency without being pushy. The product does the work.
  4. 4 Drop day. The page goes live at the exact time. Clean, fast, built to handle the traffic. Each piece has its own section with photography, specs, and a single buy button. When a piece sells, it shows as sold. That visual of watching pieces disappear in real time is part of the experience.
  5. 5 Post drop. A recap email goes out. "The drop sold out in X minutes. Here is what went. Here is what is coming next." This email does two things. It validates everyone who bought by showing them they got something exclusive. And it creates FOMO for everyone who missed it, which drives sign ups and engagement for the next drop.

That cycle repeating every two months turns your business into something people pay attention to on a schedule. It is the same psychology that makes sneaker drops and limited edition releases work, except your product is actually rare because it is handmade from a material that literally fell from space.

07 / Email is Everything

The List is the Business

This is the part that most people in your position underestimate. Instagram can change its algorithm tomorrow. Reddit can shadowban your account. TikTok can disappear entirely. Your email list is the one asset you own that nobody can take away from you.

Every single person who visits your site should have a reason to give you their email address. For the always-accessible rings, it is a purchase confirmation that puts them on the list. For people who are not ready to buy yet, it is drop notifications. "Want to know when the next drop goes live before anyone else? Put your email in."

Once they are on the list, the drop cycle I described above does the selling for you. You are not spamming people. You are giving them access to something they asked to see. That is the difference between email marketing that people hate and email marketing that people look forward to.

Your email list will become the highest revenue per subscriber of any channel you have. Every drop, you email the list. Every drop, a percentage of that list buys. As the list grows, the revenue per drop grows with it. That is the compounding effect that turns a craft into a business.
