You Built the Engine. You Just Need the Spark Plugs.

You are forging knives from 4.5 billion year old meteoritic iron with a technique that nobody else on this planet has figured out. Your pieces sell for $2,000 to $40,000 and your commissions go north of $100,000. One Reddit post with no link in it sent 30,000 people to find your website on their own.

The gap is the system that catches all of that.

You built the engine. You know what it looks like, how fast it should go, who should be driving it. What is missing is the spark plugs. The wiring that takes all of that potential energy and fires it in the right sequence so the car actually moves.

You told me about the person who was running your ads before. They were targeting demographics, household incomes, all the restrictive stuff that does not actually work. The real targeting is done in the words you use. Different personality types respond to different words, different colors, different images in significantly different ways. What resonates with one person might mean nothing to the next. You have to know what you are fishing for and use the right lure. Right now you are trying to catch a sturgeon with a worm on a hook. The fish is out there. You just need the right setup to land it.

Figuring that out should not be your job. Your job is to be at the forge. My job is to make sure the right people see your work, in the right way, through the right systems.

03 / What Your Document Already Solved

Your Tiers, Your Drops, Your Pricing. All of It.

Your three tier structure is exactly right. I want to walk through what you laid out because I want you to see how close you already are to this being fully operational.

C Tier / Always Accessible

Meteorite Rings

$249 to $549. Always on the site. This is the entry point. Someone discovers your work on Reddit or Instagram and they are not going to buy a $15,000 knife on their first visit. But they will buy a ring. And once someone owns a piece of your work, they are in your world. They are on the list. They see the drops. They move up when they are ready.

B Tier / Drops

Folding Knives, EDC, Accessories

Released every two months. Limited quantity. This is where the drop model lives. You announce it, you build the anticipation, the drop goes live, and it sells. That cycle creates a rhythm your audience starts to plan around. The scarcity is real because you physically cannot make more than a handful at a time.

A Tier / Commissions

One of One Pieces

$10,000 to $100,000+. By inquiry only. These are the pieces that end up in collections. The pieces that generate press. The Drake album cover ice pick. The meteor car. These are not sold through a checkout button. They are sold through relationships.

You had all of this mapped out in your document. The tiers, the pricing, the cadence, the constellation model for your product line. Usually when I work with a client, I am the one engineering the product strategy and then taking it to market. You already did the engineering. All that is left is the execution.

04 / The Breadcrumb Trail

All of Your Directions Go to the Same Place

One thing I noticed when I read your document is that I think you might be looking at your different product lines as separate directions. Rings go one way. Knives go another. Accessories go another. Commissions go another.

They all go the same way.

Every product you release should feel like the next chapter in a story that your audience is already following. The rings are not a separate business from the knives. The accessories are not a side project from the commissions. Each release is a breadcrumb on the same trail, and the audience follows that trail deeper into your world with every drop.

This is where the system matters. When you release a new accessory, it should not just go out as "here is the accessory line." It should be its own moment with its own build up. And the people who are ready for it should already be segmented and waiting because the system knows who they are and what they respond to. Someone who bought a ring six months ago is a different conversation than someone who found you on Reddit yesterday. Both should hear from you, but they should hear different things.

That is the only piece you have not built yet. The audiences need to be broken out. The systems need to know who goes where, who gets what, for which tier. That is what I am bringing to the table.
