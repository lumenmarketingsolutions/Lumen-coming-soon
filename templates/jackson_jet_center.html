The Artist

Tristan Dare

Twenty two years old, based in Boise, and self taught since age eleven.

Tristan forges functional art from meteoric iron that traveled through space for four and a half billion years before reaching Earth. He developed a proprietary technique that preserves the Widmanstätten crystalline pattern through forging, the kind of detail almost no one else in the world can execute, and his commission work has placed pieces in private collections across three continents.

The two pieces being made for Jackson Jet Center will be the first to incorporate components recovered from human spaceflight, with aviation becoming the material rather than the metaphor.

The Materials

What returned from orbit, fused with what came before us.

Each piece is forged from two histories: meteoric iron that fell to Earth long before humans existed, and components recovered from spacecraft humans built to leave it.

Material I

Meteoric Iron

Material that fell to Earth from interstellar space, older than the planet itself by 50 million years. The Widmanstätten crystalline pattern in this iron can only form over millions of years of cooling in zero gravity, and Tristan is one of the few smiths in the world who can preserve it through forging.

4.5 Billion Years Old · Originating Off Earth
Material II

Decommissioned Space Flown Components

Aluminum, titanium, and Inconel alloys recovered from human flown NASA spacecraft, the same materials that hold your charter customers above the ground every time they fly. Each piece will incorporate a documented component, accompanied by a certificate of provenance.

Orbit Returned · Documented Provenance

The Case

Black on black, lit from above.

Matte black millwork with museum grade glass and internal LED tuned for the temperature of steel and meteorite. The featured piece is mounted on a textured panel that echoes the surface of the moon.

The Screen

How the piece was made, looped without audio.

A vertical OLED below the case plays cinematic footage of the piece's creation. Material sourcing, the forge, and the final detail work, in long quiet takes that let anyone walking by step into the full story.

The Tag

An NFC tag at the base unlocks a private experience.

One tap walks the visitor through the story of the piece, the materials, the artist, and the path to inquire. Every scan is logged, and engagement data is reported back to JJC each month.

How It Works

What happens between someone seeing the piece and JJC receiving the commission.

A single front desk briefing of five minutes. After that, JJC's only operational role is signing for the commission wire and opening the case when a buyer wants the piece in hand.

Step I

Discover

A visitor sees the piece in the lobby and taps the NFC tag at its base, or scans the engraved code.

Step II

Engage

They reach a private experience that walks through the origin, materials, and artist, with a direct path to inquire about acquisition.

Step III

Close

We handle every inquiry directly, including conversations, terms, and payment, so JJC has no role in the sale itself.

Step IV

Handoff

The front desk receives a confirmation, opens the case, and either delivers the piece to the buyer in person or hands it to us to ship within 48 hours.

Step V

Commission

The commission is wired to JJC the same day the sale closes, without invoicing or delay, and reported alongside the scan and engagement data each month.

What We Need

Two small footprints, two power outlets, and a conversation about the rest.

Space
One placement at Boise FBO and one at Phoenix FBO. Roughly the footprint of the existing Ray Ban display in Boise, around 24 by 24 inches at the base and 6 ft tall. Final placement is determined together during a walkthrough.
Power
One standard 120V outlet at each location. For the internal lighting and the screen. Cabling is concealed inside the millwork.
Front Desk
A five minute briefing on the handoff process at each location. The desk receives a confirmation when a piece is purchased, opens the case, hands the piece over, and signs the chain of custody. That is the entirety of the operational requirement.
Build & Install
Funded, built, delivered, and installed by Lumen × DARE. Including both bespoke pieces, both cases, both screens, signage, NFC tags, screen content, and ongoing maintenance. JJC carries no cost.
Commission
Structure to be agreed in person. We have specific numbers we are comfortable with, and we would rather discuss them face to face than dictate them on paper. The intent is a structure that is meaningful for JJC and sustainable for us, paid the same day a sale closes.
Timeline
Approximately forty five days from greenlight to installation. The bespoke pieces require sourcing, forging, and finishing, with cases built in parallel. Once both pieces and both cases are ready, the install is sequenced together.
Exclusivity
First and only private FBOs in their respective regions. JJC Boise is the founding Northwest placement, and JJC Phoenix is the founding Southwest placement. As the program grows nationally, your position as founding partner is preserved.
Looking Forward

These two locations are the first of a deliberate sequence, and as the program expands across the country, JJC's two FBOs hold the founding position. What happens next is built on what you say yes to today.
