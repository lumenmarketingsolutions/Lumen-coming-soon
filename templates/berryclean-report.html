The Big Picture

We nearly tripled the conversion rate

When we launched, the landing page people saw after clicking your ad was a long, scrollable page with pricing, testimonials, and a lot of information. It looked good on paper, but most people never made it past the first scroll. They clicked the ad, hit the page, and left.

So we rebuilt it. Instead of asking people to read through everything, we dropped them straight into a quick, interactive experience. Pick your home type, answer a couple of questions, and you're done. Simpler, faster, and it worked.

What We Fixed

People were bouncing before they even started

On the old landing page, 94% of visitors left before answering the first question. They clicked your ad, saw a wall of text, and bounced. The new page gets them doing something immediately, and that changes everything.

Once someone answers that first question, they're in. The rest of the funnel holds strong in both versions. The difference is just how many people we get through the front door.

The previous ads were not bad. They got plenty of clicks. The issue was that they were pointing to the old landing page that wasn't converting. The combination of the natural talking style plus the new funnel is what's working.

The data is clear on this: the version of you that just talks, like you would to a neighbor, outperforms the scripted version every time. People scroll past polished content all day. What stops them is someone who sounds real. That first take, the one you probably thought wasn't good enough, is the one bringing in leads at $15 each.

The Biggest Takeaway

Authenticity sells more than any offer ever will

When you filmed those two versions, you said almost the exact same thing. Same message, same offer. The difference was the delivery. Take 1 had some "ums" in it and wasn't perfectly smooth, but it felt like you were just talking to someone. Take 2 was cleaner on paper, but it felt rehearsed.

People can feel the difference even if they can't name what it is. They don't care about a perfectly delivered pitch. They care about whether they trust the person talking to them. And that trust comes through when you just talk like yourself.

Here is the thing that matters most in all of this data: no discount, no special offer, no clever headline performed better than you just being you on camera. That is not a small insight. That is the entire strategy. The businesses that win long-term, the ones that become the name people think of first, they don't get there because they had the best deal. They get there because people felt like they knew them.

You want to be the biggest window cleaning company in the Treasure Valley. A good offer won't get you there. Every competitor can match a price. But nobody can copy the way you show up and talk to your community. That is the thing that scales. That is the thing that compounds over time. And the more you do it, the easier and more natural it gets.

What To Do

Just start yapping

Pick up the phone, hit record, and talk about whatever is on your mind. A job you just finished. Something funny that happened on a call. A tip about window maintenance. A thought you had driving between jobs. Don't write it out. Don't rehearse it. Don't do a second take. Just say what comes to mind and stop recording.

Not every clip will become an ad. That's fine. The point is to build the habit of just talking to the camera the way you'd talk to a customer standing in front of you. The clips that come out of that are the ones that perform. And you'll be surprised how quickly you get comfortable with it. The first few feel awkward. After that, it's just you talking.

What's Next

Where we go from here

The system is working. The funnel is converting. Now it is about putting more fuel on the fire with the right content, and letting the data tell us what to double down on.

  1. Film more content the way you filmed Take 1
    No scripts, no teleprompter. Just the topic and then you talk. First take, ship it. If you stumble over a word and correct yourself, leave it in. That is what makes it feel real. We have a few more talking points lined up and I want to get those filmed the same way.
  2. Push the new ads that are ready to go
    We have some additional creatives that haven't been launched yet. Now that the funnel is dialed in and converting at 7%, every new ad that goes live has a much better chance of bringing back leads at a strong cost.
  3. Scale spend on what's working
    Take 1 is running at $15 per lead. That is a strong number for a home service. As we add more content and let the algorithm learn, we can start increasing the daily budget and let it run. More of the same, not more complexity.
  4. Keep testing the landing page
    We tried an A/B test on the landing page but it didn't split the traffic correctly. We are going to re-run that test with a version that includes more social proof and customer reviews right on the first page. I think that will push the conversion rate even higher.

Bottom line: the ads are working, the funnel is converting, and your natural delivery is the strongest creative asset we have. We are in a good spot. Now we keep building on it.
